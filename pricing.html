How pricing and commissions work

Real estate commissions and fees can be confusing. The exact structure depends on the transaction, the market, and what you need. We explain the numbers up front so you can make a confident decision.

Our focus is not to push you into a deal. It is to help you understand total costs: purchase price, lender fees, inspections, appraisal, title, insurance, taxes, and closing costs.

Buyer costs to plan for

  • Down payment: depends on loan type and your financial plan.
  • Earnest money: shows the seller you are serious.
  • Inspection and appraisal: key steps to reduce risk.
  • Closing costs: lender fees, title, escrow, and prepaid items.

Seller costs to plan for

  • Prep work: cleaning, staging, minor repairs, and photography.
  • Concessions: items negotiated during inspection or appraisal.
  • Title and escrow fees: varies by area.
  • Moving timeline: storage and temporary housing if needed.

Can I talk with you before signing anything?

How to avoid surprise costs

The best way to avoid surprises is to plan early. We recommend getting a lender pre-approval, reviewing estimated closing costs, and setting aside a buffer for inspections and small repairs.

If you are selling, we can help you decide which improvements are worth doing before listing and which items are better handled as a negotiation credit.
